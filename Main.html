About the Festival

A Living Fire
of Culture & Faith

The Dinagyang Festival is a major religious and cultural celebration held every fourth Sunday of January in Iloilo City, Philippines. It honors the Santo Niño (Holy Child Jesus) and commemorates the historic pact between the Malay settlers and the indigenous Ati people of Panay.

Known as one of the country's largest festivals, it draws up to two million visitors each year — a breathtaking convergence of vibrant warrior costumes, rhythmic drum performances, and choreographed street dancing.
